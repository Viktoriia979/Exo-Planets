Exo Planets:

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Unlike Earth and the other planets that orbit the Sun, exoplanets revolve around distant stars throughout the universe. Scientists have discovered thousands of exoplanets, and they come in many different sizes, temperatures, and environments.

Scientists find exoplanets using several methods. One common technique is the transit method, where a planet passes in front of its star and causes a small decrease in the star's brightness. Another method is the radial velocity method, which detects tiny movements of a star caused by the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet. These techniques help scientists locate and study planets that are far beyond our solar system.

There are many different types of exoplanets. Some are rocky planets similar to Earth, while others are gas giants like Jupiter. Scientists have also discovered super-Earths, which are larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune, and ice giants that contain large amounts of frozen materials. The variety of exoplanets shows how diverse planetary systems can be throughout the universe.
